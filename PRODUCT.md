# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary user is a self-managing landlord creating a trustworthy check-in
or check-out inventory from a phone while at the property, then reviewing it
later on a phone, tablet, or laptop. They are not an inventory professional:
they need to feel capable without being asked to learn inspection jargon.

The secondary user is the tenant. They receive a focused mobile link to walk
the same evidence, raise a specific objection, and countersign. Their
experience must make agreement feel informed and fair, not coerced.

## Product Purpose

Home Inventory turns a walkthrough video or photos into a reviewed,
time-linked, signed schedule of condition. It replaces the expense and delay
of a professional clerk with a calmer evidence process that people will
actually complete. The product succeeds when both parties can understand what
was captured, verify a claim against its source, and retain a usable record
for the tenancy.

## Brand Personality

Calm, considered, evidentially rigorous.

The voice is plain-speaking and quietly authoritative. It never pretends AI
is infallible, never makes legal promises, and never turns an inspection into
a technical dashboard. It should feel like a well-prepared property file:
clear enough to use under pressure and serious enough to send to another
person without apology.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI SaaS: gradients, magic-wand language, chat-like controls,
  inflated claims, and dashboards whose numbers do not help the user act.
- Cold professional inspection software: cramped data grids, aggressive dark
  shells, tiny type, acronyms, and desktop-only workflows.
- Legalese theatre: warnings and seals used as decoration, rather than
  evidence that a person can inspect.
- Capture flows that make the user choose a technical method before they
  understand the outcome.

## Design Principles

1. **Lead with the property, not the system.** A room, a clear next action,
   and the expected outcome come before controls, models, or workflow jargon.
2. **Earn trust through traceability.** Every important claim can lead to a
   photo, a moment in video, a timestamp, and an audit record.
3. **Make completeness visible.** The user should always know what has been
   captured, reviewed, and still needs attention without reading a long list.
4. **Mobile is the field tool.** Capture, orientation, evidence checking,
   commenting, and signing must be comfortable one-handed on a modern phone.
   Desktop adds space and speed, but never becomes the only complete route.
5. **Close the file calmly.** Details, signatures, delivery, and retention
   form one legible completion ritual, with no competing "done" buttons.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA for contrast, visible focus, keyboard operation, semantic
structure, labels, and error feedback. Touch targets are at least 44 by 44px.
Never communicate status with colour alone. Respect reduced-motion settings.
Use concise, plain English and explain evidence terms in context rather than
assuming property-industry knowledge.
